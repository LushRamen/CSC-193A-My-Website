    Vietnamese Food

    Brief history: The history of Vietnamese cuisine is a rich tapestry of flavors and influences, reflecting the country's diverse history and geography. The cuisine has been influenced by neighboring countries such as China, Cambodia, Laos, and significantly by French cuisine due to French colonial rule from 1887 to 1954. The Chinese introduced several dishes to Vietnam, which were then adapted to local styles and flavors. The French introduced baguettes, which became a popular fast food in Vietnam known as 'bánh mì thịt', and other ingredients like onions, cauliflower, lettuce, potatoes, tarragon, carrot, artichoke, asparagus, and coffee. One of the most iconic dishes in Vietnamese cuisine is Phở, a noodle soup that originated in the early 20th century in Northern Vietnam. It was originally sold at dawn and dusk by itinerant street vendors and became popular in South Vietnam after the partition of Vietnam in 1954. Phở was brought to many countries by Vietnamese refugees after the Vietnam War, and restaurants specializing in Phở appeared in numerous Asian enclaves and Little Saigons, such as in Paris and in major cities in the United States, Canada, and Australia. source:https://tryotter.com/resource/wiki/what-is-vietnamese-cuisine#:~:text=History%20of%20Vietnamese%20cuisine&text=The%20cuisine%20has%20been%20influenced,to%20local%20styles%20and%20flavors.
